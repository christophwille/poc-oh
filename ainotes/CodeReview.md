* https://ckarchive.com/b/zlughnhkl759zi7qrr9qehw2zoq5qc6hlow9p  "Cognitive surrender differs in three ways. In offloading, the person remains the primary judge of what to believe or do; in surrender, the external system effectively becomes that judge, and the person’s monitoring is muted. Offloading feels like “I’m using this tool,” whereas surrender feels like “I’ve decided,” even when the person has mostly echoed an AI output. And while errors in an offloaded system tend to be caught by internal checks, errors under surrender can propagate straight into action, because Systems 1 and 2 have stood down."
